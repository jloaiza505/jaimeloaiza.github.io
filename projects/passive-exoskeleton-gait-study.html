Passive Exoskeleton Gait Study

Biomechanics & Simulation

Study on how an elastic-band ankle exoskeleton affects muscle activation during gait.

Executive Summary

OpenSim simulations were used to evaluate how elastic-band stiffness and resting length influence muscle activation during gait for a passive ankle exoskeleton.

Abstract

The study explores an elastic-band assistive device inspired by prior passive exoskeleton work. Using an RRA-optimized OpenSim model and static optimization, the effect of stiffness and resting length on plantar flexors, dorsiflexors, knee flexors, and knee extensors was quantified to find parameter values that minimize total muscle activation.

Methods

The right leg of an OpenSim model was analyzed with static optimization. An ideal elastic band geometry followed the soleus path, then a more realistic external geometry was tested.

  • Stiffness sweep: 9 values from 10 to 1000 kN/m.
  • Resting length sweep: 285, 290, 295, 300 mm.
  • Quadratic regression used to locate minima in total muscle activation.

Key Findings

  • Plantar flexor activation decreases as stiffness increases; dorsiflexor activation increases.
  • Knee extensor activation decreases with higher stiffness; knee flexors show minimal change.
  • Rest length creates a trade-off: shorter lengths reduce plantar flexor load but raise dorsiflexor demand.

Optimal Parameters (Reported)

  • Ideal geometry: stiffness about 370 kN/m, resting length about 296.96 mm.
  • External geometry: stiffness about 165 kN/m, resting length about 331.6 mm.

Discussion Highlights

The elastic band reduces plantar flexor activation during terminal stance, but increased stiffness can over-activate dorsiflexors. Geometry shifts optimal values; the external configuration reduced the stiffness required due to a longer moment arm.
